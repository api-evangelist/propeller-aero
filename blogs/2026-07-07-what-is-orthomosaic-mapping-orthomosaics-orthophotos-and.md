Orthomosaic mapping stitches hundreds of overlapping drone images into one geometrically corrected map with consistent scale and accurate positioning, turning raw aerial photos into a site view you can actually measure and build from. This guide covers what orthomosaic mapping is, how orthophotos are created, the difference between the two, and how the workflow and software fit into real construction and surveying jobs. The payoff is proof over guesswork: full-site coverage, reliable measurement, and one shared map everyone from the field to ownership can work from.
